Our Story

McDaniel work started on Royal Street in Jackson, Tennessee. From that bench, Bill built a reputation for cues that simply felt right: balanced, lively, and honest to the materials. Friends, players, and fellow makers came through the door; ideas were tested, tossed, and refined.

Today we keep the same pace. Small batches. Long conversations. Pieces that carry their history forward — from the first stroke to the next generation.

House notes:

  • We build fewer so we can build better.
  • Feel first: balance and taper lead design.
  • Provenance: every commission is documented.

Peers & Friends — Black Boar Beginnings

In 1988 the very first set of ten cues carrying the Black Boar name and logo was a true team effort — a Szamboti-inspired profile with a Black Boar twist. Tony (Black Boar) acted as ringleader; Tim Scruggs and Bill McDaniel were responsible for the blanks. The result was a run of solid-hitting cues that set the tone for what Black Boar would become.

Bill also built the first ten blanks he used to make his own first ten cues, and he and Tim remained quiet business partners for over thirty years. That lineage — ideas shared, workbench to workbench — is part of what gives McDaniel cues their unmistakable feel.

  • 1988: First ten Black Boar cues — team build (Tony, Tim, Bill)
  • Szamboti style with a Black Boar twist — balanced, confident hit
