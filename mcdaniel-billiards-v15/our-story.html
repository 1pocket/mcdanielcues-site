Heritage Reimagined: Bill McDaniel & Chase Channell

What began on pool tables across Jackson, Tennessee grew into a pursuit of the perfect hit. Master cue maker Bill McDaniel and entrepreneur Chase Channell joined forces to bring McDaniel’s legendary feel back to players who demand precision, feedback, and timeless design.

The Early Years: Bill McDaniel

Bill McDaniel grew up around the game of pool. His family owned McDaniel Amusements, a vending company operating pool tables throughout Jackson, Tennessee. As a boy accompanying his father to service the equipment, Bill would play—so small at first that he stood on pop bottle crates to reach the table.

By his teens, Bill had become one of the region’s strongest players and a regular at tournaments in the 1960s and 70s. He studied cues obsessively. Despite owning nearly every brand available, none felt perfect—so he set out to build it himself. He earned a business degree and, when astigmatism made competition harder, shifted his focus from winning racks to crafting cues.

Drawing inspiration from George Balabushka and partnering with Tim Scruggs, Bill’s early work featured four‑point short‑splice cues with veneers. He later adopted his iconic shark and stylized “MC” logos. In 1993, he introduced a shock‑absorbing ring beneath stainless joints to achieve an ivory‑like feel without the wood contacting steel. In 1994, he became an early adopter of CNC in cue making—refining both process and design through the late ’90s and early 2000s. Bill retired in 2005, leaving a legacy of artistry and unmistakable playability.

The Rebirth: Bill & Chase

West Virginia native Chase Channell grew up competing on the felt, but his path led to entrepreneurship—owning a retail paint and sundries business at 18 and rapidly rising in automotive retail. By 25, he was a General Manager in Atlanta, later relocating to Jackson, Tennessee in 2015 to lead Victory Automotive.

Even while building a career, Chase’s passion for cues never faded. He collected and tested countless builds in search of what truly played best. A mistaken online obituary briefly suggested that Bill had passed away—until a chance radio mention connected them. On December 14, 2016, they met for lunch at Logan’s Steakhouse and immediately hit it off, sharing a love for the game—especially one‑pocket—and for the craft behind a great cue.

Chase’s woodworking roots—learned from his grandfather, a pattern maker for Kelly Foundry in Elkins, WV—paired naturally with Bill’s decades of mastery. In 2019 they purchased their first lathe and set out to bring McDaniel Cues back to the billiard world, uniting heritage and innovation.

What We Stand For

We build billiard instruments with a focus on feel, feedback, and repeatable precision. Every design choice—from joints to materials to balance—pursues McDaniel’s signature hit.

  • Signature 5/16‑14 piloted joint pioneered by Balabushka and carried forward by Bill.
  • Optional 3/8‑11 flat‑faced joint (steel, phenolic, or ivory).
  • 1993 shock‑absorbing ring innovation beneath stainless joints.
  • Early CNC adoption (since 1994) paired with hand‑fit craftsmanship.
