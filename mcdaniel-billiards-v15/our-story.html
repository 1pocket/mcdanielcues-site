Our Story

A McDaniel cue is not purchased — it is commissioned. Since 1971, Bill McDaniel has built every piece with heirloom intent: hand-selected woods, surgical tolerances, and a finish meant to last decades of play.

From Jackson, Tennessee, Bill’s shop has produced cues for world champions and private collectors alike. Each billet of birdseye maple or gaboon ebony is seasoned and selected for stability. Silver and ivory inlays are set like jewelry — not just for beauty, but to balance the cue’s dynamics in the player’s hand.

Every taper is measured to the thousandth. Ferrules are turned true. The finish is cured and hand-polished until the cue feels alive on the bridge and true on the stroke. Production remains finite by design — protecting the standard that made McDaniel a name of record among cue makers.

We operate with a waiting list not to create hype, but to respect the pace of excellence. If an instrument will carry your name for a lifetime, it deserves a maker’s patience.

Lineage & Legacy

What distinguishes a McDaniel cue is not a single feature, but the sum of decisions: wood with soul, geometry with discipline, and finishes that age with grace. The result is equipment that disappears in the player’s hands — leaving only feel, focus, and confidence.

For collectors, provenance matters. Each commission is documented and delivered with care, not as inventory but as a signature. Many of our cues live in private collections and return only for cleaning or to be admired under light — which suits us just fine.
